rampage

Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense rampages , present participle rampaging , past tense, past participle rampaged pronunciation note:   Pronounced (ræmpeɪdʒ ) for meaning [sense 1], and (ræmpeɪdʒ ) for meaning [sense 2].
1. verb
When people or animals rampage through a place, they rush about there in a wild or violent way, causing damage or destruction.
Hundreds of youths rampaged through the town, shop windows were smashed and cars overturned. [VERB adverb/preposition]
He used a sword to try to defend his shop from a rampaging mob. [VERB-ing]
Synonyms: go berserk, tear, storm, rage   More Synonyms of rampage
2. 
See go on the rampage
More Synonyms of rampage

rampage in American English

(noun ˈræmpeidʒ, verb ræmˈpeidʒ, ˈræmpeidʒ) (verb -paged, -paging)
noun
1. 
violent or excited behavior that is reckless, uncontrolled, or destructive
2. 
a state of violent anger or agitation
The smallest mistake sends him into a rampage
The river has gone on a rampage and flooded the countryside
intransitive verb
3. 
to rush, move, or act furiously or violently
a bull elephant rampaging through the jungle
